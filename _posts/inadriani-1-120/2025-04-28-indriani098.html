What Is Broken Link Building and Why It Works

Broken link building is the strategy of finding dead links on other websites and suggesting your own content as a replacement. It’s like being the hero who fixes a problem and gets rewarded with a backlink.

When I first discovered this technique, I thought it sounded too good to be true. But after using it on a few client projects, I realized it’s one of the most underrated backlink strategies out there.

Why Broken Link Building Is a Goldmine for SEO

Most webmasters hate broken links. They hurt user experience and even damage their SEO. By offering a relevant fix, you’re doing them a favor while earning a high quality backlink for yourself. Win win.

1. Low Competition Opportunity

Unlike guest posting or digital PR, broken link building isn’t crowded. Most SEOs are too busy chasing the newest trends to bother fixing old problems.

When I ran a campaign targeting outdated blog posts in the health niche, the response rate was almost double compared to cold guest post pitches.

2. High Value Links

Websites willing to fix broken links are often authoritative and established. They want to maintain their reputation, which means your replacement link has to be valuable and credible.

3. Better Outreach Success Rate

People are more likely to say yes when you point out a problem and offer a ready made solution instead of just asking for favors.

How to Find Broken Link Opportunities

Finding broken links sounds tedious, but with the right tools and a little patience, it’s surprisingly doable.

  • Use SEO Tools: Ahrefs, SEMrush, and Screaming Frog can all help you scan for broken outbound links
  • Analyze Competitor Backlinks: Find broken links that used to point to your competitors and swoop in
  • Search Resource Pages: Look for resource lists or directories in your niche and check for dead links

Step By Step Broken Link Building Process

Here’s the exact process I follow when running broken link campaigns:

1. Find a Relevant Broken Link

Use Ahrefs' Broken Backlinks report or search for keywords plus "resources" and check for dead links using Chrome extensions like Check My Links.

2. Create or Identify a Suitable Replacement

If you already have an article that fits, great. If not, create a new piece specifically to fill the gap.

3. Outreach to the Website Owner

Be polite, point out the broken link, and suggest your content as a solution. Keep it short, helpful, and friendly.

Case Study How Broken Link Building Rescued a Struggling Blog

One of my clients had a food blog that wasn’t gaining any traction. We decided to test broken link building instead of the usual guest post route.

After three months, they earned backlinks from 27 high authority sites just by offering replacements for dead recipe links. Their organic traffic doubled, and they landed on the first page for several competitive keywords.

Common Mistakes to Avoid with Broken Link Building

It’s a powerful strategy, but you can still mess it up if you’re not careful. Here’s what to watch out for:

  • Suggesting Irrelevant Content: Only pitch links that truly match the original broken resource
  • Being Pushy in Outreach: Keep your tone friendly and professional, not desperate or aggressive
  • Neglecting Link Quality: Focus on reputable sites. Fixing broken links on spammy websites won't help you much

Best Tools to Make Broken Link Building Easier

You don’t have to do everything manually. Here are tools that can speed things up:

  • Ahrefs: Great for finding broken backlinks at scale
  • Check My Links: A simple Chrome extension that highlights broken links on any page
  • Wayback Machine: Helps you view what the original broken page looked like for better content creation

Final Thoughts Broken Link Building Is the Secret SEO Weapon You Should Be Using

Broken link building isn’t flashy. It doesn’t have the hype of viral campaigns or influencer shoutouts. But it works quietly and consistently to build strong SEO foundations.

If you’re willing to put in a little research and outreach effort, you can score high quality backlinks without battling a thousand other SEOs for attention.

In a noisy world of paid promotions and fake metrics, helping people fix problems is still one of the most effective ways to build real authority and trust online.
