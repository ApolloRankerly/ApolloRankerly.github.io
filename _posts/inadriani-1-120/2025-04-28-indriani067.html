What Are Editorial Backlinks

Editorial backlinks are links that websites give you naturally, without you having to pay for them or ask directly. They happen when someone genuinely finds your content valuable and decides it deserves a mention.

Think of editorial backlinks like a chef being praised by a food critic without asking for the review. It’s authentic, powerful, and sends strong trust signals to Google.

Why Editorial Backlinks Matter More Than Any Other Type

Not all backlinks are created equal. Editorial backlinks stand at the top of the pyramid because they represent real-world endorsements. When reputable sites link to your content willingly, it screams to Google, "this source is legit."

In my experience, one solid editorial link often had more impact on rankings than dozens of directory submissions or forum links combined.

How Editorial Backlinks Influence SEO Performance

Editorial backlinks deliver massive benefits for your SEO, including:

  • Authority Boost: They often come from high-trust domains
  • Relevance Signal: They are usually placed within contextually related content
  • Traffic Spike: Readers from these sites are highly engaged
  • Brand Credibility: Getting linked without asking builds genuine reputation

One time, a marketing blog cited a case study I wrote. The link didn’t just lift my page into the top five spots—it also brought in new leads who trusted me instantly because of the endorsement.

How to Earn Editorial Backlinks Naturally

It’s not about begging or buying. It’s about creating content so good, so useful, or so timely that people feel compelled to share it. Here’s what works:

  • Original Research: Conduct surveys, experiments, or studies that others will want to cite
  • Expert Roundups: Feature insights from industry leaders to encourage sharing
  • In-depth Guides: Create resources so comprehensive that bloggers prefer linking to you rather than recreating the information
  • Newsjacking: Offer timely commentary on breaking news relevant to your industry

On one project, we published a data-backed guide about social media trends. Without a single cold email, it earned over 80 editorial backlinks within the first year.

Case Study Earning Editorial Backlinks Without Outreach

While managing a SaaS blog, I wrote a monster post analyzing 500 startup websites. It was packed with stats, charts, and practical insights.

Guess what? Major industry blogs started referencing the study. Within six months, it brought in more than 120 editorial backlinks—and the best part, we didn’t ask for a single one.

The success proved a simple truth: when you create unique value, people naturally amplify it.

Common Mistakes That Stop You From Getting Editorial Links

Most sites miss out on editorial backlinks because they:

  • Focus too much on self-promotion: Nobody wants to link to a glorified ad
  • Produce generic content: If it’s been said a thousand times before, why link to it again
  • Ignore industry news: Being the first to analyze a trending topic is often rewarded with links
  • Forget about visual assets: Infographics, charts, and videos make your content more linkable

I once revamped a boring case study into a visual-heavy story format. After the redesign, editorial backlinks quadrupled without changing the actual data.

Building a System That Attracts Editorial Backlinks

Editorial backlinks can’t be forced, but you can engineer the conditions that make them likely:

  • Content Calendar: Plan regular research-based and opinion pieces
  • Proactive PR: Share new publications with relevant journalists or bloggers
  • Collaborations: Co-create with influencers and thought leaders
  • Consistency: Regular publishing builds authority over time

In my own blogging journey, having a predictable rhythm of original content led journalists to view my site as a trusted source. When they needed data or quotes, my name popped up automatically.

Final Thoughts Editorial Backlinks Are the Ultimate Trust Signal

If you want long-term SEO success, chasing editorial backlinks should be a priority. They’re harder to get, but they pay off in ways that sponsored posts or link swaps simply can’t match.

Focus on giving value first—create work that others are proud to reference. Treat editorial backlinks not as a goal, but as a byproduct of being genuinely helpful, insightful, or entertaining.

Remember, when someone links to you voluntarily, it’s not just a signal to Google. It’s a standing ovation from the web itself. And that’s priceless.
